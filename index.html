Most organizations don't have a people problem.
They have a systems problem.

Organizations become intentional one decision at a time.

Culture isn't created in an offsite. It's created through hundreds of everyday decisions:

  • how people are hired
  • how performance is discussed
  • how feedback is given
  • how leaders communicate
  • how accountability works
  • how people experience their work

These decisions don't happen independently. They're connected by the systems underneath them — and when those systems are designed with intention, the everyday decisions begin pointing in the same direction.
