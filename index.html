Phase 1: The Chaos-to-Clarity Scan

A focused diagnostic for organizations where HR is falling onto Ops, the CEO, or whoever is available.

↓

What it is

I review your existing people systems and workflows, identify the root causes of friction, inconsistency, and risk, and debrief leadership with a clear 90-day stabilization roadmap.

This is best for teams that know they have people-ops problems but need clarity on what is broken, what matters most, and where to start without overbuilding.

The 3-Week Roadmap

Week 1: Kickoff & Access A 60-minute download with the Founder, CEO, or Ops lead to hear the biggest pain points, recurring headaches, and what leadership wishes were running more smoothly. The "Mixed Signals" Scan: Review core documents such as your handbook, onboarding checklist, values statement, and recent job descriptions. Goal: Understand what the org says it does vs. what is actually written down.
Week 2: Stakeholder Pulse Calls Conversations with a diverse group of key players such as an Ops lead, a manager, and a newer hire. One Process Walkthrough: Pick one chaotic workflow — usually hiring, onboarding, or performance support — and walk through it with org leaders live. Goal: Identify where the work actually gets stuck, where leadership gets pulled in, and how the team experiences the system.
Week 3: Connecting the Dots Analyze the gap between what is documented and what is actually happening inside the organization. Draft the 90-Day Stabilization Plan with the 3–5 highest-priority actions. The Leadership Debrief: A 60–90 minute strategy session to present the Chaos Map and recommend next steps. Goal: Give leadership a clear picture of what is causing the chaos and what to fix first.

Phase 2: People Systems Launch

Build the systems your organization needs next — then embed them so they stick.

↓

What it is

Phase 2 is where we build and launch the People & Culture systems your organization actually needs — not a corporate template dropped into your Notion. Most clients come here directly after Phase 1, using the Chaos Map as their blueprint. Some come directly with a clear priority already in mind.

This is a fixed-fee, outcome-based engagement. We agree on the scope upfront, work toward a defined launch, and build in the rollout support so that what we create does not just live in a folder — it gets used.

Phase 2 is for organizations that are ready to stop improvising and put a few essential people systems in place without overcomplicating how they work.

How it works

We move through three outcomes-driven steps — no guesswork, no scope creep.

1
Define — We confirm exactly which system or systems we are building, what success looks like, who owns which decisions, and what "done" means.
2
Build + Launch v1 — We implement the minimum-viable version of the system: the one your team can actually use now, not the perfect version that gets abandoned.
3
Embed — This step covers the communications, manager enablement, and first-cycle support that makes the system stick.

What we might build

Scoped to what you actually need — not a menu you order from.

Manager operating cadence + feedback expectations — so managers know what is expected of them and teams know what to expect from managers
Onboarding system + tools — a first-90-days experience that actually prepares people to do the job
Performance-lite check-ins — simple, usable, low-admin support for teams that do not have bandwidth for heavy review cycles
Policy & process foundations — practical, not legalese — the documents your team will actually read and follow
Role clarity / leveling-lite — to reduce confusion, pay inequity, and the “is that my job?” conversations that slow everything down
Hiring structure — scorecards, interview guides, workflow — so you stop hiring on gut and start hiring on criteria
Culture & engagement listening loops — structured ways to hear from your team and act on what you hear
