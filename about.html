About

I believe intentional organizations create lasting impact.

And I've spent my career inside the organizations trying to become one.

I started this work after seeing the same pattern across growing, mission-driven organizations: powerful missions, committed teams, and people systems held together by goodwill, memory, and whoever had capacity that week.

I recognized the pattern because I had lived inside it. As the leader responsible for people and culture at an international human rights organization, I built the people function from the ground up — hiring, onboarding, performance, policy — for teams working across 13 countries. Leadership needed structure. Staff needed clarity. And the organization needed systems that matched the complexity of the work.

That experience shaped how I work today. I hold an MBA in Organizational Behavior, work in English, Spanish, German, and Portuguese, and have spent over a decade doing this work globally — most of it inside the kinds of organizations I now serve.

What I believe is simple: organizations doing mission-critical work deserve practical, fair infrastructure that carries the weight of growth — without adding bureaucracy, and without losing the humanity at the center of the mission.
