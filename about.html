About

I've seen what goodwill alone can't hold.

Why intentional organizations became my work — and what I learned building them from the inside.

My career started in the world of rules — corporate compliance. I learned early that structure without humanity doesn't work. It took longer to learn that humanity without structure doesn't work either.

That lesson landed for real when I stepped into an international human rights organization as the person responsible for its people. Almost nothing was written down. Every hire, every conflict, every departure was improvised by whoever had capacity that week. The mission was extraordinary. The team was deeply committed. And people were burning out anyway — not because anyone was failing, but because goodwill was doing a job that structure should have been doing.

So I built it. Hiring, onboarding, performance, policy — from the ground up, for teams working across 13 countries. And I watched what changed when expectations lived in writing and decisions had a known path: trust went up, escalations went down, and leaders got their time back for the work only they could do.

That is what I mean by an intentional organization. Not more process — a workplace deliberately shaped to match what it says it believes. I now spend my time helping mission-driven leaders build one on purpose, before a crisis forces the question.
